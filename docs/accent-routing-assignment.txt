
#Speech Problem Framing Exercise: Accent Routing

In this exercise, we want to understand how you approach a realistic speech problem where the main difficulty is not just model training, but defining the problem well, choosing sensible evaluation criteria, and making practical decisions under ambiguity.

The problem statement:

* Detect whether a speaker belongs to a target accent category, and define a practical way to use that signal in a speech product.

You may use any tools, libraries, pretrained models, and AI assistants you like.

### What to do

Please spend about 4 to 5 hours (tops) on the exercise and prepare the following:

* A short written problem definition:
  * What exactly are you predicting?
  * What counts as success or failure?
  * What assumptions are you making?
* A baseline approach, using any pretrained model, heuristic, or open-source component you think is reasonable.
* One meaningful improvement over that baseline. This could come from better task definition, better slicing, better thresholding, better data selection, or a better modeling choice.
* A small prototype, notebook, or script that demonstrates both the baseline and the improved approach.
* A short evaluation plan and results summary:
  * What data would you use?
  * What metrics would you report?
  * What edge cases matter most?
  * What did you learn from the comparison?
* A brief discussion of failure modes, open questions, and what you would do next with another week.
* A short note on how you used AI tools:
  * What you delegated
  * What you accepted
  * What you corrected or rejected
  * How you validated the output

### Constraints

* Do not spend time training a large model from scratch unless you believe it is necessary.
* It is completely acceptable to use pretrained or open-source models.
* We care more about your judgment than about building the biggest system.
* If the problem is ambiguous, make reasonable assumptions and state them clearly.

### Deliverables

Please submit:

* A short write-up, around 2 to 3 pages or equivalent
* A notebook, repo, or script
* A short results summary, which can be included in the write-up
* Any instructions needed for us to run the work
* A brief summary of how you used AI tools

### What we will look for

We will look for:

* How well you define the task
* Whether your evaluation setup matches the actual problem
* Whether you identify important failure cases
* Whether your implementation choices are pragmatic
* Whether you use AI tools thoughtfully rather than passively

### Interview format
~ 75 minutes 
First 30 minutes: questions about what I have done
Remainder (except introductions): talk through scenarios and collaboratively develop creative solutions
